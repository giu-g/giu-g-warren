Section 3:

Challenges

Another conclusion...

The most significant challenge I faced this quarter was moving past broad, generalized conclusions in the final stages of my writing. In early drafts of both projects, when it came time to wrap up my arguments, I found myself defaulting to general statements like "climate change is a bigger issue than this" or calling for vague, collective action. After spending pages building specific, localized arguments (such as analyzing structural educational deficits in Brazil), my conclusions initially flattened that complexity.

I fell into a comfortable pattern of zooming out.

I realized this tendency happens for two reasons, the first being that it serves as an "easy out." Writing an actionable conclusion is incredibly difficult, and it is much easier to fall back on familiar rhetoric about global responsibility than it is to propose realistic next steps. Furthermore, time constraints played a massive role; unpacking a "wicked problem" takes time, and time is not on our side in a quarter system.

Refining these conclusions is the exact place I would have directed my focus, if I had more time.

More Revision!

A second significant challenge I faced this quarter was learning how to meaningfully revise my writing. In my previous writing course (WCWP 10A), revision was relatively minimal. Most of my changes were surface-level grammar edits, and I only truly altered one scene.

In this course, my professor’s method of leaving comments on the essays forced me to slow down and reread my essays with more intention. I had to actually explain my reasoning, and clarify my claims, which made the process more demanding, but also much more meaningful.

At the same time, I noticed that I often avoid fully engaging with my own writing during revision because I feel somewhat embarrassed reading my early drafts. And confronting my writing directly feels very strange, so I tend to limit myself to smaller edits, even though I know it has the potential to significantly improve my ideas.

I can still see room for growth in how I engage with feedback. I left five revision comments on Project 1 and four on Project 2, which reflects that I was beginning to actively respond to my own writing, but also that I did not yet fully push myself to the level of depth possible during revision.

Moving forward, I see this as an area I want to strengthen!
